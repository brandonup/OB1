# Your Open Brain Use Cases

*Personalized for Brandon — solo builder splitting time between the Leakey Foundation and AI product development (Kinetic, Brain), with a growing consulting practice.*

---

## Save This (Preserving What AI Helps You Create)

These are moments when AI produces something worth keeping — a framework, a reframe, a prompt approach — that you'd want to reference later instead of rebuilding from scratch.

**1. System prompt breakthroughs for Kinetic or Brain.**
You're iterating on a system prompt in Claude and finally land on a version that nails the tone or behavior you want. Instead of losing it in conversation history, capture the reasoning behind why it works.
> *"The system prompt pattern that finally got Brain to handle ambiguous queries well was giving it a decision tree before the persona instructions. Ordering matters more than wording."*

**2. Architecture decisions with rationale.**
You work through a technical decision in Claude Code — say, how to structure the Qdrant collections, or how Supabase auth should flow. The decision sticks, but the reasoning disappears.
> *"Decided to keep OpenAPI spec as source of truth and generate FastAPI routes from it rather than the reverse. Reason: prevents AI coding assistants from introducing breaking changes when the codebase grows."*

**3. Consulting positioning language that clicks.**
You're refining how to describe your AI consulting practice to SMBs and a particular framing resonates — the "humans as orchestrators of AI agent fleets" angle, or spec-writing as your differentiator.
> *"Best framing for SMB consulting pitch so far: 'I don't build AI tools for you — I figure out which problems are actually worth solving with AI, then write the spec so any developer can build it right.'"*

**4. Cowork workflows that solve real problems.**
You figure out an effective pattern for using Cowork with Leakey Foundation work — like how to take a Zoom transcript and extract action items reliably. That workflow is worth preserving.
> *"Cowork workflow for Leakey meetings: paste Zoom transcript → ask for action items by stakeholder → cross-reference against existing task list in Linear → flag anything new or contradictory."*

**5. AI learning insights that shift your mental model.**
You're spending 3-4 days a week learning AI and occasionally hit an insight that reframes how you think about building. Those are the captures that compound.
> *"Key insight from this week: RAG retrieval quality matters 10x more than model choice. Spent two days improving the model when the real issue was chunking strategy."*

## Before I Forget (Capturing While It's Fresh)

These are moments where information is fresh but will decay fast — post-meeting details, decisions made on calls, gut reactions.

**1. After every Leakey Foundation call or meeting.**
The AI/Evolution dinner has many moving parts and stakeholders. After a call with Sharal or another stakeholder, the specific commitments, concerns, and open questions are perishable.
> *"Call with Sharal today — confirmed venue capacity is 120, she wants the AI demo segment kept under 15 minutes, and the keynote speaker's travel hasn't been booked yet. She's worried about the seating chart for donors."*

**2. When a stakeholder changes a requirement mid-project.**
Someone on the Leakey project shifts a deadline or changes a deliverable, and you need to remember not just the new requirement but who said it and when.
> *"Leakey event update: the printed program needs to go to the printer by Friday not next Monday. Sharal confirmed via email 4/3. This moves up the content review deadline."*

**3. After Upstart Collective conversations.**
You're still getting to know your AI colleagues. When someone shares an insight, a tool recommendation, or their area of expertise, that's context worth banking.
> *"Talked with [name] at Upstart today — they've been doing fine-tuning work for healthcare clients. Good person to ask about domain-specific model customization."*

**4. When you notice a pattern in your own work.**
You're learning new systems constantly to keep up with AI's pace. When you notice something that keeps tripping you up, or a learning approach that works, capture it before the metacognition fades.
> *"I keep trying to learn new AI tools by reading docs first. What actually works is: find one specific task, do it, then read the docs to understand what I did. Doing-first learning."*

**5. Consulting lead conversations.**
As you start getting more advisory/consulting work, early conversations with potential clients are full of context — what they care about, their actual problems, their budget signals.
> *"First call with [prospect] — 12-person marketing agency, currently using ChatGPT for copywriting but no system around it. They want 'AI strategy' but what they actually need is workflow design. Seemed price-sensitive."*

## Build the Thread (Compounding Over Time)

These are topics where daily captures accumulate into something more valuable than any single note.

**1. AI/Evolution dinner — project evolution log.**
This event has a one-week runway with many stakeholders. Capturing the daily state — what changed, what's at risk, what's confirmed — builds a project narrative that prevents things from slipping through cracks and creates a post-event retrospective for free.
> *Daily capture: "AI/Evolution status 4/6: Program content finalized. Still waiting on AV quote. Seating chart draft 1 sent to Sharal for review. Risk: printed program timeline is tight."*

**2. AI learning journal.**
You're spending half your week learning AI. A running thread of what you're learning, what tools you're trying, what works and what doesn't, becomes a personal knowledge base that compounds. In three months, you'll have a curated map of what you actually know vs. what you've only skimmed.
> *Weekly capture: "This week I learned about structured outputs in Claude, tried Cursor for the first time (verdict: good for small edits, Claude Code still better for architecture), and realized I need to understand embeddings better for Brain."*

**3. Consulting practice development.**
Every conversation, every pitch attempt, every piece of feedback about your consulting offer is a data point. Over time, this thread reveals your real ICP, your best positioning, and the objections you need to handle.
> *Ongoing capture: "Noticed that when I say 'AI consulting' people assume I'm selling chatbots. When I say 'AI operations design' they lean in. Need to keep testing language."*

**4. Relationship building at Upstart Collective.**
You don't know your AI colleagues well yet. Regular small captures about who knows what, who's working on what, and what you've discussed will turn a loose network into a real professional community in your memory.
> *After each interaction: "Had a good conversation with [name] about agent orchestration. They recommended checking out CrewAI. They're building something in the legal space."*

## Surface the Next Best Move (Proactive Prioritization)

This is the pattern where your brain stops being passive storage and starts working *for* you — using everything it knows about your projects, deadlines, stakeholders, and goals to highlight what you should do next, especially when you're heads-down and losing the forest for the trees.

**1. When you're deep in Kinetic code and the Leakey event is a week away.**
You get absorbed in a technical problem for hours. Meanwhile, there's a stakeholder email you haven't responded to, a vendor confirmation that's overdue, or a deliverable that's about to miss its window. Your brain should be able to tap you on the shoulder.
> *Proactive nudge: "You've been in Kinetic code all morning. The AI/Evolution printed program goes to the printer Friday — has the content review been finalized? That's higher urgency than this feature work."*

**2. When you're spending time on a lower-leverage activity without realizing it.**
You're tweaking a system prompt for the third hour when the highest-value thing you could do today is follow up with a consulting prospect who expressed interest last week. Your brain has both data points — it should connect them.
> *Proactive nudge: "You captured a note about [prospect] being interested in a proposal last Tuesday. It's been 5 days with no follow-up. That's probably higher ROI than prompt optimization right now."*

**3. When accumulated context reveals a deadline collision.**
You've been capturing project updates across Leakey, Kinetic, and consulting — and your brain can see that two deadlines are converging on the same day, even though you planned them in separate contexts.
> *Proactive nudge: "Based on your recent captures: the Leakey event program deadline and the Kinetic sprint demo are both landing on Friday. You might want to front-load the program work today."*

**4. When a stakeholder is waiting and you've lost track.**
Sharal asked you something three days ago and you captured the ask but never captured the response. Your brain notices the open loop.
> *Proactive nudge: "Sharal asked about the AV setup options on 4/3. There's no follow-up captured. Is that still open?"*

**5. When your weekly pattern shows you're neglecting a goal.**
You said you want to spend more time getting consulting work, but your captures over the past two weeks are 90% Leakey and Kinetic. Your brain can reflect that back to you.
> *Proactive nudge: "Your last 14 days of captures show zero consulting-related activity. You flagged this as a priority — what's one small move you could make this week?"*

## Your Daily Rhythm

**Morning (before deep work begins):** Ask your brain "What's the most important thing I should do today?" Let it look across your accumulated context — project statuses, open loops, stakeholder asks, neglected goals — and surface the highest-leverage next move before you get pulled into reactive work. Then do a 60-second "state of play" capture to keep the thread building.

**After every Leakey call or stakeholder interaction:** Capture the 2-3 things that changed, were decided, or are now at risk. This is where Cowork shines — let it extract from transcripts, then capture the synthesized version to your brain.

**End of AI learning sessions:** Before you close out a Claude conversation or wrap up a learning block, ask yourself: "What's the one thing I figured out today that I'd want future-me to know?" Capture it.

**Friday wrap-up:** One capture that summarizes the week across all your contexts. What moved, what's stuck, what do you need to remember for Monday? This becomes your Monday morning launch pad.
